Find out what English and maths training your apprentice should do

  1. Does the apprentice have at least Level 2 English and maths?
    This is the same as grade E or 2** at GCSE.
    • Yes: They do not need to do any more English or maths training.
    • No: Continue to question 2.
  2. If you answered ‘no’ to question 1, do the apprentice’s English and maths qualifications meet the standard or framework of their apprenticeship?
    • Yes: They must also do Level 2 English and maths.
    • No: Continue to question 3.
  3. If you answered ‘no’ to question 2, does the apprentice have a special educational need, learning difficulty or disability?
    • Yes: Continue to question 4.
    • No: Continue to question 5.
  4. If you answered ‘yes’ to question 3, will the apprentice be able to get the minimum requirement in English and maths?
    • Yes: {{_endPointOne}}
    • No: We’ll fund functional English and maths Level 2 for the apprentice. They must get at least entry Level 3 functional English and maths.
  5. If you answered ‘no’ to question 3, does the apprentice have at least approved Level 1 English and maths?
    • Yes: {{_endPointOne}}
    • No: Continue to question 6.
  6. If you answered ‘no’ to question 5, is the apprentice working below Level 1?
    • Yes: They must get Level 1 and 2 in English and maths. We may fund these qualifications, based on an assessment of the apprentice. {{_endPointOne}}
    • No: They must do Level 2 English and maths (which we’ll fund). If they cannot achieve this, they must get Level 1 English and maths (which we will not fund). They must get the minimum required standards in English and maths.
