Guidance

Funding for training apprentices in England

Published 25 June 2018
From:
Education and Skills Funding Agency

Who and what can be funded

Government funding is available to cover most of the costs associated with off-the-job training for apprentices.

A government-approved provider must carry out the training on behalf of the apprentice’s employer.

Costs, and the amount of funding available, vary depending on the type of apprenticeship.

For an employer to get funding for their apprentice, they must agree the price of training with their chosen provider before the apprenticeship starts. This total price must include any assessments of the apprentice’s skills and knowledge.

To be eligible for funding, apprentices must:

  • have the right to live and work in the United Kingdom (UK)
  • have been living in the UK for at least 50% of the 3 years prior to starting their apprenticeship
  • be at least 16-years-old
  • spend at least 50% of their working hours in England for the duration of the apprenticeship
  • be doing an apprenticeship at a higher level than qualifications they already hold
  • not be doing another apprenticeship or be in receipt of any other further education funding during their apprenticeship programme
  • not benefit from funding for any part of their programme that duplicates training or assessment they have received from any other source

The price of apprentice training includes:

  • off-the-job training that covers the knowledge, skills and behaviours needed for the apprentice to complete the apprenticeship
  • registration, examination and certification costs associated with mandatory qualifications
  • assessments and progress reviews
  • classroom sessions
  • self-directed distance or online learning
  • training materials and equipment needed to complete the apprenticeship
  • administration costs directly linked to training and assessment
  • time spent by other staff supporting apprentices that is directly linked to the apprenticeship training and assessment in addition to generic line management responsibilities
  • additional learning requirements such as basic English and maths
  • costs for re-sitting exams linked to a mandatory qualification
  • end-point assessment re-takes
  • accommodation costs if the apprentice needs to do any training away from home
  • costs for taking part in a skills competition if it directly contributes to helping the apprentice achieve an apprenticeship
